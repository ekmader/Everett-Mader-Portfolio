An Abundance of Braces

Sustainability in Tradition

A timber framing project centered on structural joinery and brace design during a collaborative build.

Challenge

When I first became interested in architecture, one of my professors told me that before I could design buildings, I should learn how they’re made. That advice led me to Scratch Builders, a specialty frame wood shop in Vermont known for doing everything in-house, from design drawings to raising the last frame.

I joined as a carpenter to learn what drawings cannot teach, like how Douglass Fir does not consider your feelings when it splinters through a finger, or the degree of coordination and trust that is required to make something stand. For one house my main role was to measure, cut, and finish thirty-four timber braces for a frame that would be installed in the course of a few days.

Each brace is hand-crafted to fit the specific structural requirements, ensuring both strength and aesthetic beauty in the final timber frame.

The collaborative nature of timber framing brings together multiple craftspeople, each contributing their expertise to create a unified, structurally sound whole.
