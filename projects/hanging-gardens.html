Hanging Gardens Cafe

Reframing a transitory site into a destination for presence, connection, and ecological awareness.

A 250 m² café concept blending greenhouse architecture with communal dining and natural immersion.

Site Concept

The Hanging Gardens Café transforms a marginal, transitory urban lot into a place of respite and ecological engagement. The design is intentionally porous, encouraging serendipitous encounters between strangers.

Vegetation is treated as both program and structure, mediating light, temperature, and acoustic comfort while providing a layered, sensory experience for patrons.

Program

The café supports flexible seating, a greenhouse kitchen, and a series of elevated planting beds that double as social thresholds. The material palette emphasizes reclaimed timber and simple metal joinery.

Operational strategies prioritize seasonal adaptability and minimal energy systems driven by passive solar design and natural ventilation.

Materiality

Locally-sourced timber and salvaged glazing keep embodied carbon low, while providing a warm tactile quality to the interior spaces.

Spatial Sequence

Movement through the café alternates between open green rooms and sheltered niches, creating moments of discovery and pause.

Community

Programming encourages programming by locals: seed exchanges, pop-up markets, and workshops that activate the space beyond typical café hours.

Details

Construction details emphasize simple connections that can be repaired locally. The café is meant to evolve through time with the community.
